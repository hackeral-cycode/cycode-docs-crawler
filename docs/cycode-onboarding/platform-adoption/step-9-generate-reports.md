# Step 9: Generate reports

[](https://gitlab.com/cycodehq/documentation/cycode_docs/-/blob/main/src/content/cycode-onboarding/platform-adoption/step-9-generate-reports.md "Edit this page")

Updated on May 26, 2024 • Published on May 23, 2024

* * *

As your teams begin to work in the Cycode platform, you can now [generate reports](/additional-cycode-features/service-level-agreement-sla/sla-reports) to see how they’re doing using the Reports feature, which enables you to automatically generate and export data for Violations, Discovery queries and [SBOM reports](/dashboards/scanner-dashboards/sca-dashboard) . The Reports feature includes tools for automating reports, scheduling report generation, and setting notifications about reports to relevant stakeholders.

Follow these steps to create your first report:

1.  Go to the Reports page and click the **Create Report** button.
2.  Fill in the name of the report, for example “Projects at risk”.
3.  Fill in the description of the report, for example “weekly report to list the projects that have critical or high open violations”.
4.  Click next, choose the **Custom (query base)** option and click the **Next** button again.
5.  Select a query from the saved queries list, for example “Cycode projects with critical or high violations in public assets” (you can find it using the search bar or “Risk Best Practices” category) and click **Next**.
6.  Select the report format, for example CSV, schedule the report to be sent on a weekly basis, and select to get notification to your email.
    1.  Note that the email selection is based on the emails defined in the email integration in the integration page.
7.  Click the **Create Report** button and see the new report appear in the reports table.
8.  The report shows as “Pending” and will change its status to “Available” in a few minutes.
9.  Once the status of the report changes to “Available”, download the report and view it.
