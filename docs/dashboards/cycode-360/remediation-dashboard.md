## Overview [Copied!](#overview "Copy link to this section")

The Remediation dashboard helps you track remediation efforts, measure effectiveness, and keep your organization on track to meet its security goals. It highlights key metrics such as **Detected vs. remediated** violations, performance trends over time and **SLA status** in the widgets described in this article.

* * *

## Widgets [Copied!](#widgets "Copy link to this section")

### Available Fixes & Open Tickets [Copied!](#available-fixes--open-tickets "Copy link to this section")

This widget provides a snapshot of two critical remediation metrics:

Widget

Description

Available Fixes

The number of automated fixes generated by the platform, ready for application.

Open Tickets

The total number of tickets from external ticketing systems that are linked to identified violations.

### SLA Status [Copied!](#sla-status "Copy link to this section")

This widget uses a pie chart to display the current status of your team’s adherence to [service level agreements (SLA)](/additional-cycode-features/service-level-agreement-sla) for security remediation, as defined by the system administrator.

The statuses are categorized as:

*   **On Track:** Violations addressed within the defined timeframe.
*   **Near to Be Missed:** Violations approaching the SLA deadline.
*   **Missed:** Violations that exceeded the SLA timeframe.

SLAs are based on the severity of the violations, with specific timeframes assigned to ensure timely remediation.

### Detected VS Remediated [Copied!](#detected-vs-remediated "Copy link to this section")

Shows the number of detected violations versus remediated violations over time.

It provides a visual representation of how effectively your team is addressing security issues, allowing you to monitor trends and measure progress. This widget helps ensure that remediation keeps pace with detection to maintain a strong security posture.

### Delta [Copied!](#delta "Copy link to this section")

The Delta section highlights the difference between detected and remediated violations, helping you assess the balance between detection and remediation efforts.

*   **Detected VS Remediated Delta:** Shows whether the current difference is positive, negative, or neutral, providing a quick status check.
*   **Detected VS Remediated Delta Over Time:** Tracks the trend of this difference to help you monitor progress and identify patterns.

This section provides valuable insights into whether your remediation efforts are keeping up with new detections, ensuring a proactive approach to risk management.

### MTTR (Mean Time to Resolution) [Copied!](#mttr-mean-time-to-resolution "Copy link to this section")

The MTTR section provides a comprehensive view of how efficiently your organization resolves security violations. It includes three widgets:

*   **MTTR by Risk**  
    Displays the current Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR) in days, broken down by risk levels (info, low, medium, high, and critical). This widget highlights how long it takes, on average, to resolve violations of varying severities.
*   **MTTR Over Time**  
    Tracks changes in MTTR over time, offering insights into trends and improvements in resolution efficiency through a visual line chart.
*   **MTTR vs. SLA**  
    Compares the MTTR to the defined SLA timeframes (in days) for each risk level. This widget helps identify areas where remediation efforts are aligned—or misaligned—with SLA standards.

Together, these widgets provide a detailed analysis of your organization’s remediation efficiency, helping you evaluate performance and ensure timely risk mitigation.

### Top 5 of Ignored and Resolved Policies [Copied!](#top-5-of-ignored-and-resolved-policies "Copy link to this section")

These widgets highlight the top 5 policies with the highest number of ignored violations and resolved violations, respectively.

*   **The Top 5 Ignored Policies widget** helps you identify policies that are frequently ignored, offering insights into areas that may require adjustments or improved enforcement.
*   **The Top 5 Resolved Policies widget** showcases policies that have contributed most significantly to resolving violations, helping you recognize effective remediation efforts.

### Total Prevented, Detected, Remediated Violations [Copied!](#total-prevented-detected-remediated-violations "Copy link to this section")

This section provides a breakdown of violations across three key stages of the remediation lifecycle:

*   **Prevented Violations:** Violations that were proactively blocked, primarily in pull requests, before being released, categorized by their source.
*   **Detected Violations:** Displays violations identified within the system, broken down by risk score, offering insights into the volume and severity of identified risks.
*   **Remediated Violations:** Includes all resolved or ignored violations within the selected timeframe, highlighting the organization’s effectiveness in addressing security issues.

More about _Prevented Violations_

These represent violations identified in pull requests that could have been avoided if addressed by the developer, helping to maintain code quality before release. The choice to act on these warnings remains with the developer.

By comparing detected and remediated violations, this section helps evaluate your organization’s ability to address security issues effectively and maintain a strong security posture.

### Performance Over Time [Copied!](#performance-over-time "Copy link to this section")

This widget presents a month-by-month breakdown of your organization’s security performance over the past year, displayed as a bar chart. It tracks trends in prevented, detected, and remediated violations, providing a clear view of how these metrics evolve over time.

* * *

General Note:

**Reopening a violation:** When a violation is resolved or ignored and subsequently reopened (which can happen multiple times), the trend data will only reflect the first remediation date and time, regardless of how many times the violation was reopened and addressed. This approach ensures the accuracy of the trends, focusing on the actual number of violations managed through Cycode.

**Disabling a policy:** Disabling a policy can reduce the number of open violations reported, as any violations associated with the disabled policy will no longer be counted.
